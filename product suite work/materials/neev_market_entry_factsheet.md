# Neev Market Entry — Product Factsheet

**"Enter equity on your terms"**

Date: March 2026 | Version 1.0

---

## Investment Objective

Neev Market Entry systematically deploys your Reserve corpus into large-cap equity using valuation-aware pacing. Instead of blind SIPs that ignore market conditions, Market Entry buys more when markets are cheap and slows down when they're expensive — delivering smoother entry into equity with lower drawdowns than a standard Nifty SIP.

---

## Investment Strategy

1. **Reserve base**: All surplus first flows into Neev Reserve (20% liquid + 80% arbitrage)
2. **Deployment trigger**: Once Reserve exceeds 3x monthly surplus, excess begins flowing into equity
3. **Monthly deployment**: 20% of excess Reserve is deployed each month
4. **PE-based pace modulation**:
   - Nifty PE < 16: **2x bonus** deployment (deep value)
   - Nifty PE < 18: **1.5x pace** (cheap market)
   - Nifty PE 18–22: **1.0x pace** (normal)
   - Nifty PE > 22: **0.5x pace** (expensive market)
5. **Target fund**: UTI Nifty 50 Index Fund — Direct (pure large-cap index exposure)
6. **Continuous rebalancing**: Undeplyed surplus stays in Reserve earning 7%+

## Ideal For

- **First-time equity investors** who want systematic, valuation-aware entry
- **SIP investors** who want smarter deployment than blind monthly purchases
- **Moderate risk tolerance**: Comfortable with small drawdowns (-2.5%) for meaningfully higher returns
- **Investors with lump sums** who want to avoid timing risk — Market Entry deploys gradually based on valuations
- **Stepping stone**: Natural progression from Reserve before moving to Accelerate/Navigate

---

## Key Risks

1. **PE-based timing may underperform in sustained bull markets**: If markets stay expensive (PE > 22) for extended periods, deployment slows and you may miss upside. The 2020-21 bull run partially demonstrates this
2. **Concentrated index exposure**: 100% of equity allocation goes to Nifty 50 — no mid-cap, small-cap, or international diversification
3. **Valuation metric limitations**: Nifty PE is a backward-looking metric and may not capture structural changes in market composition or earnings quality
4. **Behavioral risk**: Seeing Reserve corpus deplete as it deploys into equity during corrections may cause anxiety, even though this is the system working as designed
5. **Lower absolute returns than full equity exposure**: The Reserve base + gradual deployment means Market Entry will always trail a fully-invested equity portfolio in strong bull markets
