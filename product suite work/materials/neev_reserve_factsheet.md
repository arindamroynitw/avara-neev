# Neev Reserve — Product Factsheet

**"Your money's best parking spot"**

Date: March 2026 | Version 1.0

---

## Investment Objective

Neev Reserve replaces your savings account and fixed deposit as the default home for idle cash. It targets 7%+ annual returns with zero drawdown risk and same-day liquidity, using a tax-advantaged blend of liquid and arbitrage funds.

Every rupee that isn't actively deployed into equity lives here — earning 2x what a savings account pays, with equity-class tax treatment on the arbitrage portion.

---

## Investment Strategy

1. **Surplus sweep**: Monthly surplus (after expenses) and initial idle cash flow into Reserve, minus a ₹30,000 bank floor for daily expenses
2. **Allocation**: 20% into a liquid fund (Parag Parikh Liquid) for T+0 access, 80% into an arbitrage fund (Kotak Arbitrage) for higher yield
3. **Liquid cap**: Liquid fund capped at ₹2L to minimize debt-tax exposure; excess flows to arbitrage
4. **Monthly rebalancing**: Portfolio rebalanced to 20/80 target each month
5. **Tax advantage**: Arbitrage funds receive equity taxation (12.5% LTCG after 1 year) despite delivering debt-like returns

---

## Key Risks

1. **Credit risk**: Liquid fund holds short-term debt instruments; arbitrage fund holds hedged equity positions. Both are among the lowest-risk MF categories, but not government-guaranteed like bank deposits
2. **Arbitrage spread compression**: If market volatility drops, arbitrage yields may fall below FD rates temporarily
3. **Exit load**: Kotak Arbitrage charges 0.25% exit load on redemptions within 30 days. FIFO lot tracking minimizes this
4. **Tax law changes**: The equity LTCG treatment on arbitrage funds depends on current tax classification. Reclassification to debt taxation would reduce post-tax returns
5. **Inflation risk**: At 7.20% pre-tax, real returns are ~1–2% above inflation — sufficient for parking, not for long-term wealth creation
