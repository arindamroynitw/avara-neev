**"Grow faster with curated multi-asset allocation"**

## Investment Objective

Neev Accelerate is the active growth engine of the Neev suite. It deploys surplus beyond Reserve into a diversified, multi-asset portfolio with curated active funds, momentum-based satellite rebalancing, and quarterly optimization — targeting alpha over market indices while maintaining disciplined risk management.

---

## Investment Strategy

1. **Reserve base**: All surplus first flows into Neev Reserve (20% liquid + 80% arbitrage)
2. **Deployment trigger**: Once Reserve exceeds 3x monthly surplus, 25% of excess deploys monthly into the diversified portfolio
3. **Core allocation (40%)**: Nifty 50 index — stable large-cap foundation
4. **Satellite allocation (60%)**: Spread across 4 actively-managed or specialized funds (15% each):
   - ICICI Nifty Next 50 — large-cap breadth
   - HDFC Midcap Opportunities — mid-cap alpha
   - Parag Parikh Flexi Cap — multi-cap + international
   - Motilal Oswal S&P 500 — US equity diversification
5. **Quarterly momentum rebalancing**: Every quarter (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec), satellite weights are tilted toward funds with stronger recent momentum and away from laggards
6. **Momentum tilt factor**: Moderate (2x, clamped to 0.5x–1.5x range) — enough to capture trends without excessive concentration

## Key Risks

1. **Active fund underperformance**: HDFC Midcap and Parag Parikh Flexi may underperform their benchmarks in future periods. The 54 bps alpha over Navigate is slim and may not persist
2. **Survivorship bias**: The active funds were selected with knowledge of their historical track record. Future fund performance is uncertain
3. **Momentum tilt risk**: Quarterly momentum rebalancing can amplify losses if recent winners reverse sharply
4. **Mid-cap concentration**: 15% allocation to HDFC Midcap adds volatility and liquidity risk during market stress
5. **International currency risk**: S&P 500 allocation is subject to USD/INR exchange rate fluctuations
6. **Higher drawdown than Market Entry**: -6.25% max drawdown vs -2.47% for Market Entry — the price of higher diversification and returns
7. **Expense ratios**: Active funds have higher expense ratios than index funds, creating a structural drag vs Navigate
